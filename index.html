Arithmetic Operators

Arithmetic operators are used to perform mathematical operations such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. You might have used these operations in mathematics before, and now we’ll see how they work in Python.

There are 7 arithmetic operators in Python:

  • Addition (+): Adds two numbers.
  • Subtraction (-): Subtracts the second number from the first number.
  • Multiplication (*): Multiplies two numbers.
  • Division (/): Divides the first number by the second number.
  • Modulus (%): Returns the remainder of the division.
  • Exponentiation (**): Returns the first number to the power of the second number.
  • Floor Division (//): Divides the first number by the second and rounds down to the nearest whole number.

Order of Operations

Order of operations, also called the order of precedence, is a set of rules that determines the sequence in which different operations are evaluated in a mathematical expression. Just like BODMAS in mathematics, we have PEMDAS.

P: Parenthesis ()

E: Exponentiation **

M: Multiplication *

D: Division /, //, %

A: Addition +

S: Subtraction -

Parentheses are performed first, then exponentiation, multiplication, and division in the same order of precedence. These are evaluated from left to right, meaning the one at the left will be performed first. Lastly, we have addition and subtraction, which also have the same order of precedence and are evaluated from left to right.

From left to right means, if two operators have the same order of precedence, the one at the left will be performed first, then the next one to its right, and so on.
